Geography

1. By what name did the Romans call the city of Bath in England?
a. Aquae Sextiae b. Vercellae c. Aquae Sulis d. Camolodunum

2. The Turkish town of Hissarlik is the site of what ancient city?
a. Troy b. Alexandria c. Sidon d. Sparta

3. The cities of Tiryns and Mycenae are located on which of these?
a. Attic plains b. Peloponnesus c. Pontus Euxinus d. Island of Crete

4. Which ancient city is correctly matched with its modern name?
a. Narbo – Nimes b. Eboracum – York
c. Gades – Cannes d. Mediolanum – Madrid

5. Which of these ancient cities was likely to be one’s destination having departed from Brundisium?
a. Carthago Nova b. Londinium c. Deva d. Athenae

6. Which modern place did the Romans call Caledonia?
a. Ireland b. Scotland c. Belgium d. Austria

7. In which Roman province was the city of Cordoba located?
a. Lusitania b. Tarraconensis c. Baetica d. Narbonensis

8. The Roman province of Dacia is roughly equivalent to the geography of which Eastern European
nation?
a. Bulgaria b. Romania c. Serbia d. Poland

9. The Italian town of Padua was known as ______________ to the Romans.
a. Padus b. Patavium c. Panormus d. Pisae

10. Which city in Sicily is farthest west?
a. Syracuse b. Eryx c. Agrigentum d. Lilybaeum

11. Lutetia was founded on which river?
a. Liger b. Rhodanus c. Rhenus d. Sequana

12. Which of these islands is farthest north?
a. Lemnos b. Lesbos c. Skyros d. Chios

13. Which of these Roman provinces did not border the Mediterranean Sea?
a. Mauretania b. Cappadocia c. Aegyptus d. Achaea

14. In traveling from Rome to Tarentum, what direction would one venture?
a. northeast b. southwest c. northwest d. southeast

a. Thessalonica b. Sparta c. Corinth d. Delphi

a. Mare Hyrcanum b. Mare Nostrum
