1. The Muses are said to reside on what sacred mountain?
a. Mt. Pelion b. Mt. Parnassus c. Mt. Ida d. Mt. Helicon

2. Which sorcery goddess was, according to Hesiod, Zeus’ bringer-of-victory?
a. Circe b. Hecate c. Eurybia d. Nyx

3. Upon being gored by a wild boar, into what kind of flower did Adonis turn?
a. Peony b. Anemone c. Hyacinth d. Daffodil

4. After Jupiter flooded humanity, what early goddess of prophecy told Deucalion & Pyrrha
to “throw the bones” of their mother?
a. Metis b. Theia c. Phoebe d. Themis

5. Which Titanic couple are the parents of three of the four winds?
a. Hyperion & Theia b. Thaumas & Electra
c. Crius & Eurybia d. Eos & Astraios

6. Who was tricked by Mercury into tattling about Apollo’s stolen cattle?
a. Agenor b. Marsyas c. Battus d. Mopsus

7. Speaking of tattling, who tattled to Pluto about Persephone eating the pomegranate
seeds and was turned into an owl as punishment?
a. Alpheus b. Arethusa c. Aeacus d. Ascalaphus

8. Who was the step-mother of Theseus, who tried to kill him in favor of her own son?
a. Medea b. Ariadne c. Phaedra d. Electra

9. For Achilles having murded Diomedes’ cousin, Thersites, Diomedes dragged which
beautiful Amazonian queen behind his chariot?
a. Polyxena b. Pyrrha c. Penthesilea d. Polydora

10. Life After The Iliad, Part One: Diomedes was exiled from Argos. He eventually ends up
in Italy, marries a princess, and is deified after his death. What place was that?
a. Apulia b. Praeneste c. Capua d. Tarentum

11. What mythological creature was decapitated by Perseus?
a. Chrysaor b. Sphinx c. Medusa d. Euryale

12. Against whom did Odysseus hold a grudge for endangering baby Telemachus and
causing Odysseus to reveal he was, in fact, sane—dragging him into the Trojan War?
a. Daunus b. Palamades c. Eginus d. Orestes

his brother, Polynices, into exile?
a. Tydeus b. Adrastus c. Parthenopeus d. Eteocles

a. Achelous b. Scamander c. Inachus d. Tiberinus

a. Eurydice b. Isis c. Syrinx d. Daphne

a. Phineus b. Cepheus c. Acrisius d. Leukippe
